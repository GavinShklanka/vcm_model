Key Findings & Insights

Algorithm Superiority

XGBoost outperformed the Logistic Regression baseline and Random Forest in terms of AUC-ROC, demonstrating its capability in capturing non-linear interactions across environmental and behavioral factors.

Primary Risk Factor

SHAP analysis indicates high wind speeds and temperature fluctuations consistently elevate severe collision probability, acting as the primary macro-drivers of severity beyond standard exposure.

Regime Volatility

The Non-Holiday sub-model yielded the highest predictability (AUC=0.487), while the Night-time sub-regime proved most challenging (AUC=0.438), indicating varied driving behaviors under low visibility.

Exposure Normalization

Aligning raw collisions against Vehicle Miles Travelled (VMT) successfully neutralized bias from high-volume highways, proving the framework reliably measures severity probability.

Pipeline Methodology

  • Data Merging: Combined synthetic collision reports, weather station observations, and monthly VMT volumes on exact `location_id` intersections.
  • Feature Engineering: Generated interaction terms (e.g., VMT × Visibility) and boolean regime flags for night-time, severe weather, and holidays.
  • Modeling Strategies: Evaluated Logistic Regression (baseline), Random Forest, and XGBoost with GridSearchCV hyperparameter tuning over stratified 5-fold CV.
  • Interpretability: Permutation importance and SHAP analysis measured the definitive marginal impacts of predictors across identical test distributions.
  • Behavioral Isolation: Trained independent XGBoost classifiers conditioned on the 6 different driving regimes.
